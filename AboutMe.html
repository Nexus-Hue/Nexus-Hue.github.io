    Nexus Hue Backstory

    I was born in Tijuana but raised in Nayarit. While in Nayarit I lived with my grandparents in the house my grandpa made. I remember him working on the house as I lived there, and I feel like seeing that had a big impact on me. I’ve always liked trying to get things done on my own as well, and I feel like it’s in part thanks to him & my mom. This is also around the time I started drawing because I wanted to make cool posters like the ones my aunt Rocio made.

    When I was around 7 years old, I came to live with my mom here in Fresno. At first, I found it hard to make friends because I didn’t know English. This made me hella shy, and even after I learned English drawing was usually still my main way to meet new people. This kept me consistently drawing until I got into making videos. In the 6th grade, I started recording videos with an old phone my mom didn’t use anymore and started uploading them to YouTube. I mostly recorded without a purpose, then started vlogging on Facebook in middle school.

    While in highschool I spent most of my time skateboarding and making videos. I was really into editing my lines and slams to make them funny. This got me buying broken iPhones on Craigslist, to fix & sell for profit to fund my “projects.” (and also because I kept breaking my phone skateboarding lol) The first screen I fixed was my own after I broke it skating lol

    I was inspired to do this by watching my mom make money out of fixing and flipping furniture online. If I didn't have her as a role model, I probably wouldn’t be as willing to keep learning new skills and trying new things. The first iPhone I bought and fixed was purchased using the money I made from flipping furniture with my mom. I went with her to yard sales and would buy small things like instruments or video games. Eventually, I literally sold a couch I got from the free section of Craigslist for $345. That was a crazy feeling, and I honestly still feel weird about it lmao. With that money & other sales I bought myself a laptop and a DSLR that I used for more videos.

    When I graduated highschool, however, I didn’t even consider graphic design or video as a career. The thought of working as any kind of designer, editor, or especially animator felt completely out of reach. Just thinking about it made me extremely depressed. Like no matter what I did I would never be able to catch up to all the talented artists on the internet. Having this in mind I went to Fresno State undeclared, and eventually dropped out. I basically gave up on school for a cool minute but had a wake-up call when I injured my neck badly. While hurt I felt more like a burden to everyone than a helping hand. Working with art, however, has helped me a ton. No matter what I'm doing I always seem to end up connecting it back to art. To me Nexus Hue means bringing everything together and I can't see myself doing anything else.<\P>
