  • Spherical videos

    (360° videos)

    I wanted to test out 360° videos as quickly as possible to learn its limitations before I get started on a larger project. This is why I decided to make it low resolution(1080s) without sound. The scene was rendered using unreal engine 4.27 and the sequence was exported as a video using the free version of Davinci Resolve. After exporting the video, I injected the VR metadata and uploaded it to YouTube. It can be watched using a VR headset, but I feel like the video is best suited for watching on a phone due to the quality. I also recommend a phone, because a computer/laptop would lose the ability to move around by physically turning around with your phone. (although you can still click and drag the screen to look around)

    I hope to use this type of video for storytelling or music videos (most likely both together). Since they are 360°, it would be impossible to see the whole video by just watching it once. This might encourage the viewers to watch them again just in case they missed something. Since Youtube’s algorithm favors re-watchability, I think this could help the videos gain some additional views. I also like the idea of using this to showcase products like digital art or for fashion to demonstrate ideas using 3d models instead of using a 2d sketch.

  • Real objects for WEB 3.0

    (Photogrammetry)

    During winter break I learned photogrammetry and have been wanting to use it for videos. As of right now, I have only scanned small objects like hats, shoes, and an Mr2 for use in animations. I plan to scan locations to bring into unreal engine 5 for hyper realistic scenes. These scans could also work well with 360 videos to make virtual museums. I was also thinking I could make a tutorial to share some of my tips and tricks on photogrammetry.

  • What I learned from the MVP

    (Prototype animation)

    The video did better than I thought it would on Instagram. It managed to reach just under 5k views. Which isn’t that much but not bad for a smaller account. According to the insights, account reach went up by %98,380 and it managed to get me 8 new followers. As far as potential customers I have no idea if anyone who saw it would be interested in being part of one of those animations. Although 17 people liked it enough to save it with a bookmark which I thought was cool.

    In contrast, the video did really poorly on YouTube, getting only 20 views. My guess is that it could be due to the copyright strike. The song I used was available to use for YouTube shorts, but I used the instrumental version which might have been the reason for the copyright strike. So, in conclusion, YouTube might work better for tutorials since I know lots of people including myself. Use YouTube as their main source for tutorials. Althought I will probably still try uploading another similar animation as a Youtube short to be sure. TikTok did okay but I was honestly expecting it to do the best on there since the app is music-related. In comparison to Instagram, it was not very good, but I also only have 2 followers on there. My Instagram reel might have done better thanks to the people who shared it and that got others to see it and share it as well.
