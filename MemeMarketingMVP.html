    "Get Bread", First VFX mvp
    • 3D Animation
    • March 11, 2022

    This video is my first public prototype for VFX compositions. This video didn't do as well as the marooned prototype in terms of views, but the view to like ratio is still better. I also think I learned some very useful marketing tricks from this test.

    I was testing if Instagram pushes videos that are simply original (visually as well as the sound (Original tag not song, hence the distortion for the test)). This came to mind after reading an article where they linked a video from Instagram stating they will be “ranking for originality.” Therefore, I decided to make a reel to test prices instead of re-posting my past projects as a compilation. (Click here for article)

    I feel like holding the viewer’s attention is still more important because of Gen Z. A younger audience is more likely to share videos, and this makes it more likely that Instagram will promote your content making it reach the people who you want to see it. For one, this video is missing that initial hook like the marooned video. In the marooned video I kept introducing new things into the scene little by little to keep the viewer interested. This video was an attempt at meme marketing, but has less emphasis on keeping your attention, and more work was put into the actual design of the set.
